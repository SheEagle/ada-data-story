Phase 0: General Analysis

Take a Look at Reddit’s Galaxy

Welcome to the Reddit Galaxy. In this vast digital expanse, every Subreddit acts as a unique planet. These worlds do not exist in isolation; they are tethered by "gravitational bridges"—hyperlinks that allow information and users to travel from one corner of the universe to another. To understand this cosmos, we first calibrated our instruments to capture the basic signals of inter-community interaction.

Our initial scan revealed two distinct frequencies of communication: Title and Body mentions. The Title frequency is significantly more active, generating approximately 572,000 links across 54,075 unique planets—nearly double the volume and 1.5 times the variety found in Body mentions (287,000 links). Interestingly, these two frequencies never overlap; every signal is a unique star-bridge, suggesting that most interstellar dialogue begins with a high-impact "broadcast" in the title rather than within the deeper "crust" of the post body.

As we tracked this galaxy over a 2.5-year period (2014–2017), we observed a steady, linear expansion ($R^2 = 0.87$). This is not a chaotic growth, but a rhythmic pulse. Perhaps most encouragingly, the "spectral analysis" of these links shows a galaxy dominated by light: Positive sentiment links outnumber negative ones by a staggering 8.35 to 1. Furthermore, positive connections are expanding at a much faster rate (slope 2.39) than negative ones (slope 0.24), indicating that as the Reddit Galaxy grows, it is becoming increasingly defined by constructive alignment rather than friction.

Zooming out to view the entire topological map, we identified 67,180 planets interconnected by 339,643 bridges. On average, each planet maintains roughly 10 gravitational ties to others, creating a densely populated social space. However, the connectivity is not uniform. We discovered a "Great Super-cluster"—a massive, weakly connected structure encompassing over 97% (65,648) of all known planets. Outside this main civilization, about 711 tiny "Islands" float in deep space, consisting of only a handful of planets that remain disconnected from the galactic hub.

This directed network reveals a clear hierarchy: while the galaxy is physically massive and largely reachable, the flow of influence is often one-way. This directional gravity shapes how information cascades through the system, moving from influential "super-giants" to the peripheral settlements of the outer rim.

Research Question 01

Based on community social attributes, what distinct communication patterns can we define?

When we first approached Reddit as a data “galaxy” of this scale, the most intuitive strategy was straightforward: to examine what these individual “planets” (subreddits) were talking about. We began with a set of content-based embedding vectors representing each community, which functioned much like a resource map—highlighting where discussions of technology, entertainment, or shared topical interests were concentrated.

However, it quickly became clear that a purely content-driven perspective was insufficient. Two communities that frequently discuss “Bitcoin” are not necessarily close in any meaningful social sense: one may be a long-standing supporter community, while the other exists primarily to criticize or mock it. Treating them as neighbors solely on the basis of shared vocabulary obscures the more consequential social structure beneath. Content captures what communities talk about, but it offers limited insight into whom they align with.

For this reason, we shifted our focus away from the content map and toward a more fundamental layer of the system: the hyperlink structure connecting communities. Each citation, cross-post, or reference represents a point of contact between subreddits—whether as endorsement, borrowing, critique, or confrontation. Using these links, we constructed an inter-community network and applied the Leiden clustering algorithm to identify cohesive “constellations” within it. Compared to traditional classification methods, Leiden is particularly effective at detecting groups that are structurally dense and interactionally cohesive, making these constellations a closer approximation of Reddit’s operative social topology.

Once this structural star map was established, a new challenge emerged: how to characterize the “social distance” between communities. A binary distinction between interactions occurring within the same constellation and those crossing constellation boundaries proved too coarse, as it could not account for why some cross-community exchanges remain measured and constructive while others escalate rapidly into hostility.

To address this, we further partitioned all interactions into four categories, which form the analytical foundation of our subsequent analyses:

Figure 1.2 - Distribution of Link Categories: Quantifying the four interaction modes within the Reddit Galaxy.
PROFILE 01
Intra-Core Interactions

Intra-Core interactions occur between the most tightly connected communities within a constellation; these communities share a highly aligned discourse and normative framework, and their exchanges tend to be more nuanced and internally coherent.

PROFILE 02
Intra-Periphery Interactions

Intra-Periphery interactions also take place within the same constellation but involve more peripheral communities, where shared cultural background exists alongside weaker ties, often creating conditions for internal friction.

PROFILE 03
Cross-Strong Interactions

Beyond constellation boundaries, we distinguish Cross-Strong interactions, which connect communities from different constellations that nonetheless maintain stable, recurrent exchanges, typically supported by a partial shared context.

PROFILE 04
Cross-Weak Interactions

Finally, Cross-Weak interactions occur between communities with little or no prior relationship, representing the greatest social distance and the highest likelihood of contextual mismatch and emotionally charged communication.

The purpose of this four-way classification is not to introduce additional labels, but to more precisely examine how language and conflict vary with social distance.

Research Question 02

How do the social attributes of communication links—defined by intra-community roles and cross-community connection strength—determine baseline linguistic patterns in a peaceful context?

In the previous stage, we conceptualized Reddit as a galaxy of tens of thousands of communities and identified four fundamental interaction types: Intra-Core, Intra-Periphery, Cross-Strong, and Cross-Weak.

With Research Question 02, we move beyond describing the structure itself and ask a more focused question: Once major confounding factors are removed, how does social distance shape language use?

This question seems straightforward but is methodologically delicate. Raw data cannot directly answer it, as they are heavily influenced by covariates. Without proper control, any patterns observed might reflect noise rather than genuine effects. The goal here is not simply to “discover patterns” but to systematically calibrate the data and determine which linguistic differences are truly driven by social distance.

First Calibration: Controlling Conflict Rate

The first source of distortion is the uneven distribution of conflict across interaction types.

Conflict is not evenly spread in the Reddit galaxy. Cross-Strong interactions show a much higher frequency of antagonistic language than internal community interactions. By contrast, Intra-Core and Intra-Periphery interactions are generally more restrained. Analyzing all messages together would allow high-conflict language to mask the structural properties of everyday communication.

To address this, we divide the corpus into peaceful and conflictual contexts. This is not to diminish the importance of conflict but to ensure that structural language patterns reflect social distance rather than emotional escalation. In this analysis, we focus first on the peaceful context, where language choices reliably reflect communicative expectations and audience perception.

Second Calibration: Controlling Source Clusters

Even within peaceful interactions, a second confound exists: the Source Cluster.

Each cluster in the Reddit galaxy consists of communities with similar topics, norms, and stylistic habits. These inherent preferences influence language. Some clusters favor long sentences, abstract concepts, and extended reasoning; others prefer short, direct, and pragmatic communication. If a specific interaction type is dominated by certain clusters, cluster-specific style could be misattributed to social distance.

To remove this bias, we apply a Linear Mixed-Effects Model (LMM). Each message’s source cluster is treated as a random effect, absorbing cluster-level stylistic differences. The remaining fixed effects therefore more reliably reflect the influence of interaction type itself. Effectively, the LMM allows us to observe language in the Reddit galaxy after subtracting local cultural coloration.

Research Question 03

To what extent does the transition from a peaceful to an antagonistic context trigger differential linguistic adaptations across distinct link types

If the Reddit galaxy under peaceful conditions revealed an elegant equilibrium shaped by stable orbits, conflict acts as a sudden gravitational disturbance. Using the LMM model on conflict-context data, we observed that antagonism does not merely amplify emotions—it fundamentally reshapes the structure of signals across the galaxy.

We focus on Cross-Strong links—the connections that, even during disputes, attempt to maintain a form of “diplomatic stability”—to track how social distance distorts communication under conflict.

Language Structure Under Conflict: Four Dimensions

DIMENSION 01
Complexity: Sentence Structure and Signal Density
  • Intra-Core: Previously complex and high-resolution signals simplify, switching to direct, less layered messages.
  • Intra-Periphery: Shorter sentences and higher capitalization highlight emphasis to ensure signals stand out amid noise.
  • Cross-Strong: Maintains medium complexity, balancing readability for cross-cluster understanding.
DIMENSION 02
Logical Style: Organization and Alignment
  • Cross-Strong: High proportions of functional words, articles, and prepositions indicate structured, aligned communication designed to prevent misinterpretation.
  • Intra-Core: Increased use of certainty words signals assertive and position-driven messaging.
  • Intra-Periphery: Reduced cognitive processing words show reasoning is less explicit, reflecting raw, emotionally-driven discourse.
DIMENSION 03
Identity Expression: Pronoun Usage
  • Intra-Core: Strong increases in “I,” “We,” and “They” show conflict has mobilized identity, turning discussion into a defense of self and group.
  • Cross-Strong: Elevated “They” usage keeps attention focused on external clusters, sustaining inter-cluster orientation.
  • Intra-Periphery: Pronoun changes are less pronounced, indicating weaker identity-driven mobilization.
DIMENSION 04
Emotional Energy: Intensity and Disruption
  • Intra-Periphery: Swearing (+72.10%) and anger (+32.62%) highlight raw, emotion-driven outbursts.
  • Intra-Core: Swearing also rises (+19.32%), revealing that even the galaxy’s core can erupt when internal conflict emerges.
  • Cross-Strong: Emotional indicators remain low, showing rational debate persists despite surrounding turbulence.

Research Question 04

Macro Perspective: Steady-State Structures and Conflict-Driven Reorganization in the Reddit Galaxy

Up until now, we’ve explored how different communication links behave at the micro-level, analyzing signal complexity, logic, identity markers, and emotional tone. But a galaxy isn’t defined by individual signals alone—it is the interplay of all its stars and clusters. At a macro scale, we face a structural question: What fundamentally defines the social architecture of the Reddit galaxy? When conflicts erupt, does this architecture remain resilient, or does it collapse and reorganize?

To address this, we adopt a multi-dimensional LMM coefficient vector framework, which allows us to track the stability and divergence of interaction patterns across different social contexts. This framework provides a way to map the galaxy’s structural “gravity wells” and observe how they shift under conflict.

1. Initial Hypothesis: “Mirror-Constant” Structures

In the exploratory Phase 0, we observed that the Reddit galaxy is dominated by positive interactions: roughly 8.35 positive links for every negative one. This led to an intuitive hypothesis:

Positive interactions define the social skeleton of the galaxy. They determine who occupies the core, who bridges clusters, and who orbits the periphery. Conflicts may add turbulence, but they should not fundamentally reshape this skeleton—at most, they change the “color” of the network without altering its structure.

In other words, the galaxy’s structural backbone, formed by the majority of positive links, was expected to remain mirror-constant, stable across both friendly and adversarial interactions.

2. Technical Verification: Peace as the Galaxy’s “Anchor”

To test this, we compared two perspectives: Global Perspective (including all interactions) and Purely Positive Perspective (filtering out conflicts).

Methodology & Observations

We extracted the fixed-effect coefficients for 27 language features, producing high-dimensional behavior vectors for each link. Computing Pearson correlation coefficients revealed near-perfect alignment: peripheral links (Intra-Periphery) demonstrate correlations as high as 0.99.

This implies that positive interactions are not just dominant in volume—they define the steady-state social architecture of the Reddit galaxy. Peace acts as the galaxy’s gravitational anchor, holding core and peripheral clusters in predictable orbits.

3. Structural Divergence: Conflict-Induced Logic Shifts

When we shift to a purely negative perspective, the mirror-constant assumption breaks. Conflict does not simply amplify existing patterns—it creates a new social gravity, one that reshapes the galaxy’s landscape.

Structural Drift: High-dimensional behavior vectors losing alignment under pure conflict contexts.

Observations show that correlations shrink significantly under conflict. Peripheral regions (Intra-Periphery) show the largest divergence, indicating that clusters at the edge of the galaxy are the first to experience structural destabilization. Conflict warps the underlying social skeleton, producing behaviors unseen under steady conditions.

4. Role Reorganization: Deceptiveness of High-Traffic Links

Why do seemingly dominant core links lose influence in conflict? LMM intercept and coefficient shifts reveal that high traffic does not guarantee structural power.

Relegation

Core Cross-Strong links appear central in global models due to sheer interaction volume, but lose influence in specific contexts.

Bubble Effect

Their influence depends on a majority of non-negative interactions—essentially a traffic bubble that bursts under conflict.

Reshuffling

In a pure conflict context, previously peripheral connections rise, demonstrating structural role reshuffling.

5. Conflict vs. Peace: Macro Patterns

Peace Defines the Rules

Positive interactions established the structural framework of clusters, cores, and bridges. This framework is remarkably resilient under normal conditions.

Conflict Drives Divergence

Negative interactions trigger structural drift. Peripheral clusters destabilize first, and cross-cluster links may lose authority.

Dynamic Role Shifts

High-traffic core links are powerful under peace but are vulnerable to conflict due to their reliance on positive interactions.

Reshuffling

The conflict environment elevates peripheral and opportunistic clusters, challenging the established hierarchy.

Conclusion: The Reddit galaxy is a dynamic equilibrium system, where peace defines the steady-state social order, and conflict drives localized reorganization. Influence in calm conditions does not guarantee influence under pure antagonism—social gravity is context-sensitive, and roles can invert quickly.
