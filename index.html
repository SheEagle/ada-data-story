Take a Look at Reddit‚Äôs Galaxy

Welcome to the Galaxy of Reddit : a massive social networking platform, known for its vast communities (subreddits) on discussions, news, expert advice, entertainment (memes, stories), and various niche topics. In Reddit's vast digital expanse, every planet ( Subreddit) pools together likeminded thinkers from across the galaxy. These planets do not exist in isolation. Discussions on Reddit forums can take various forms and encompass all kinds of emotion (positive, negative, angry, appreciative, etc.). We, the geeky inhabitants of planet r/EPFL, are fascinated by these information exchange patterns and want to understand how the linguistic style of communication varies for different links within Reddit's online galaxy. To observe them, we calibrated our instruments to spy on these intra-planet links!

Our initial scan revealed two distinct styles of intra-community links. Some users embed the links to other communities' posts deep within the body of the post, while others mention it in the title itself. Stanford's Reddit Hyperlink Network record all such hyperlinks from 2014 to 2017. Using analytical tools granted by the galaxy‚Äôs data analysis overseers, ADA, we explored these hyperlinks and uncovered interesting (and sometimes unexpected) information pathways.

The Expanding Reddit Universe

The Reddit Hyperlink Network dataset contains approximately 860,000 hyperlinks from one community to another, over a period of 2.5 years, with title citations forming two thirds of the total 572,000 links across 54,075 unique subreddits in the dataset. In this 2.5-year period, the weekly number of posts mentioning other subreddits grew at a steady pace, following a strong linear trend (R2 = 0.87) (Figure 0.1), with most posts encompassing positive or neutral sentiment. (The ratio of positive/neutral sentiment to negative was 8.35 : 1!) Furthermore, linear regression reveals a substantially steeper growth rate for positive and neutral mentions (slope = 2.39) than for negative mentions (slope = 0.24) (Figure 0.2).

Our structural scan reveals that this expanding universe is not perfectly connected. While a few crowded planets constantly exchange messages, vast regions remain silent or isolated. Research Question 01

How can we categorize links based on Social Roles and Social Distance?

Community aggregation

Since social networks are known to exhibit community structures, hence to better understand the dynamics of communication between subreddits, we first need to identify and aggregate them into larger communities based on their interaction patterns. For this, we used three widely used community detection algorithms : Leiden, Infomap, FastGreedy. To evaluate their performance we used:

  • üî∏ Conductance: Captures how much interaction ‚Äúleaks‚Äù across cluster boundaries; lower values indicate sharpercommunity structure, more stable borders between star systems.
  • üî∏ Coverage: Indicates the proportion of total interactions in the galaxy that are internal to the clusters.

While FastGreedy achieves the highest modularity score, its optimization favors dense internal grouping at the cost of slightly higher boundary leakage. Infomap, in contrast, fragments the galaxy into over three thousand micro clusters, producing a structure that is overly fragmented.

The Leiden algorithm offers the most balanced results. With the lowest average conductance (0.198), it identifies 85 clusters while maintaining adequate coverage of overall interactions. For this reason we use it in further analyses as main clustering algorithm.

Thus, we cluster communities based on how frequently they interact with or mention other subreddits!

A Two Stage Classification Framework for Community Reference Links

How do we categorize the links based on the subreddit role and their social distance?

  • Stage 1: Within-cluster Link Classification
    Within each community cluster, communities are ranked by weighted degree (total connection strength) to identify their influence levels:
    • Core Communities: The top 30% most active hubs, serving as discussion centers within the cluster.
    • Peripheral Communities: The remaining 70%, positioned at the social periphery.
    Based on this, we define the first two groups:
    • Intra-Core: Links initiated by core communities targeting other members within the same cluster.
    • Intra-Periphery: Links initiated by peripheral communities targeting other members within the same cluster.
  • Stage 2: Cross-Cluster Link Classification
    For references spanning different clusters, tie strength is measured via interaction frequency (edge weight). Using the median as a threshold, we define the remaining two groups:
    • Cross-Strong: Cross-cluster links with interaction frequency above the median, indicating close connections between the groups.
    • Cross-Weak: Cross-cluster links with interaction frequency below the median, representing occasional ‚Äúbridge‚Äù interactions between otherwise separate social domains.
Distribution of links does not fall in equal proportions, that core planetary cluster is really socially tightly connected. Top 30% of most connected together planets really send a lot of reports about each other.

Research Question 02

How do social roles and social distance within the Reddit network influence the linguistic patterns and emotional tone of interactions in a peaceful context?

üìã Core Terminology

To ensure analytical clarity, we operationalize our definitions based on the Reddit Hyperlink Network attributes:

  • Interaction, Communication & Message: These terms refer specifically to the act of one community referencing another via a directed hyperlink.
  • Peace Context: References with positive or neutral sentiment (POST_LABEL = 1), representing the network's baseline steady state.
  • Conflict Context: References with negative sentiment (POST_LABEL = -1), representing inter-community friction and social pressure.

In the previous stage, we defined four types of links: Intra-Core, Intra-Periphery, Cross-Strong, and Cross-Weak. But before we move further with our analysis, we need to take into account sentiment type and cross-cluster differences.

Controlling conflict rate

Reddit hyperlinks can be of positive/neutral sentiment or negative sentiment. Negative references are not evenly distributed across subreddits. For example, Cross-strong links contain a disproportionately high number of negative references, while intra-periphery links contain the fewest; cross-weak and intra-core links show similar levels of positive/neutral and negative links. Pooling all references would therefore let negative links disproportionately drive the aggregate results. To control for this bias, we analyze negative and positive/neutral references separately by creating separate graph structures with links of a certain sentiment only, and repeating the process of clustering and link-type classification for each graph.

Fig 2.1 Conflict Rate Comparison in 4 Groups.

Controlling cluster specific differences

Each cluster in the Reddit galaxy consists of communities with similar topics, norms, and stylistic habits. These inherent preferences influence language. Posts from some clusters might favor long sentences, abstract concepts, and extended reasoning; others might prefer short, direct, and pragmatic communication. If a specific interaction type is dominated by certain clusters, cluster-specific style could be misattributed to social distance. To limit this bias, we use a Linear Mixed-Effects Model (LMM). Interaction type is modeled as a fixed effect, using Cross-Strong links as the reference category, or baseline with which we will compare the values for all other link types. This reduces the influence of cluster-specific language patterns and allows the fixed effects to capture relative differences associated with link type.

After breaking down Reddit interactions along several communication dimensions, such as linguistic complexity, identity signals, and emotional tone, we can step back and look at the bigger picture. Together, these patterns suggest that communication in the Reddit whithween social groups does not happen randomly. Instead, it tends to cluster into a small number of recurring styles shaped by who speaks (social roles) and how far is the subject about which a refrence is made (social distance).

Each profile can be seen as a different kind of star system in the galaxy: some are dense and stable, some sit on the edges, and others act as bridges between distant clusters. These profiles do not describe individuals, but rather the typical communication patterns that emerge under different social positions and distances.

Four Communication Profiles

Intra-Core

Messages initiated by core members, often showing higher readability and slightly longer sentences. These interactions may indicate that core members tend to share more detailed and context-rich information within familiar social space.

Intra-Periphery

Interactions dominated by peripheral members, characterized by shorter sentences and a much higher use of capitalization. This pattern may indicate a stronger reliance on visual emphasis to gain attention or assert presence.

Cross-Strong

Cross-cluster interactions between socially closer clusters, regardless of whether the speakers are core or peripheral members. These links tend to show moderate complexity and more explicit structure, which may indicate an effort to maintain clarity when talking about someone beyond one's immediate cluster.

Cross-Weak

Long-distance cross-cluster messages between socially distant clusters. These messages show more dispersed linguistic features and less consistent structure, which may indicate higher uncertainty and weaker shared context when refering to distant social space.

Research Question 04

How does the global network architecture differ when constructed using only positive versus only negative links, and does conflict based network drive a reshuffling of community centrality and influence?

So far, we have examined Reddit interactions at the micro level, focusing on language complexity, logical style, identity expression, and emotional tone. However, a galaxy is not defined by individual signals alone. Its structure emerges from the collective motion of stars and clusters. At the macro level, we therefore ask a structural question: What defines the social architecture of the Reddit galaxy, and what happens to this architecture when we only use conflict links?

All previous analyses were conducted on the global network, where positive and negative interactions were jointly aggregated. This perspective implicitly assumes that conflict behaves as a local disturbance within an otherwise stable system. To test this assumption, we deliberately reconstruct the galaxy under two different observational lenses: a peace network containing only positive interactions, and a conflict network containing only negative interactions.

Our analysis follows a clear, step-by-step pipeline:

  1. Extract interaction texts and sentiment labels from the raw Reddit dataset.
  2. Split the global interaction graph into a peace network (positive links only) and a conflict network (negative links only).
  3. Compute 27 linguistic features for each interaction, including readability metrics, sentence structure, pronoun usage, emotional tone, and LIWC categories.
  4. Fit linear mixed-effects models (LMMs) to estimate fixed effect coefficients, producing high dimensional behavioral vectors for each interaction type.
  5. Compare structures across contexts using Pearson correlation, Jaccard similarity, and feature drift analysis.

This pipeline allows us to observe the Reddit galaxy at scale: identifying its stable gravitational skeleton, and tracking how conflict perturbs or reorganizes existing orbits.

1. Peace Network as the Galaxy‚Äôs Anchor

Earlier we observed that positive interactions dominate: roughly 8.35 positive links per negative one. This leads us to think that positive interactions define the Reddit galaxy‚Äôs ‚Äúgravitational skeleton,‚Äù determining which subreddits occupy the core, which serve as cross-cluster bridges, and which orbit at the periphery. Conflicts may induce local turbulence, but the overall skeletal structure should remain stable. To rigorously test this, we compared the global network with the peace-only network:

  • Computed LMM fixed-effect coefficients for each link, resulting in 27-dimensional high-dimensional behavior vectors.
  • Calculated Pearson correlations between corresponding links in the peace and global networks.
  • Observed near-perfect alignment: peripheral links (Intra-Periphery) reached correlations up to 0.99, with core links similarly aligned.

4. Conflict vs. Peace: Macro Patterns

Peace Network as Anchor

Positive interactions form the framework for clusters, cores, and bridges, which remains robust under normal conditions.

Conflict Drives Drift

Negative interactions cause structural drift; peripheral clusters destabilize first, and some cross-cluster links may weaken.

Dynamic Role Shifts

High-traffic core links are strong under peace but vulnerable in conflict due to their minimal negative interactions.

Reshuffling

Conflict causes elevation of peripheral and opportunistic clusters, challenging the established hierarchy.

Conclusion: The Reddit galaxy is a dynamic equilibrium system. Positive interactions establish a stable social structure, anchoring communities into predictable roles. Conflict, in contrast, drives structural drift, destabilizing established cores and enabling the rise of previously peripheral nodes. Influence on Reddit is therefore context dependent: prominence in peaceful discourse does not necessarily translate to dominance in conflict, and network hierarchies can rapidly reshuffle under antagonistic conditions.

Epilogue

Final Reflections

Our voyage through the Reddit Galaxy reveals a profound truth: the structural "where" dictates the linguistic "how." Online communication is not a chaotic void, but a sophisticated ecosystem governed by social gravity and structural roles.

Structure as Destiny

Communication patterns are not random. Whether a community is a Core hub or a Periphery satellite determines its baseline "voice": from the complex reasoning of the center to the attention-seeking signals of the edges.

The Contrast of Conflict

Conflict acts as a revealer, sharpening the distinctions between social roles. In a Conflict Context, the gap between groups widens: the Periphery erupts in raw, unstructured emotional flares, while the Core uses logic and group identity to defend their position.

Systemic Reshuffling

Influence is context-dependent. A star that shines brightly in the Peace Network may lose its gravity in the Conflict Network. Antagonism doesn't just change behavior; it triggers a systemic reshuffling of the social hierarchy.

The Galaxy Endures

As the Reddit Universe continues to expand, the tribes within it will keep orbiting their shared interests. Some will act as bridges (Cross-Strong), maintaining logic amidst the noise; others will remain as isolated signals (Cross-Weak). By understanding these linguistic echoes, we gain more than just data‚Äîwe see the invisible social architecture that connects us all in the digital expanse.
