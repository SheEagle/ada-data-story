Introduction

People naturally form communities around shared interests, yet because our interests differ, we end up belonging to several overlapping social circles. As our participation shifts among them, we transfer the information we perceived from one to another. Experiences drawn from one context are used to justify points or share ideas in others, but this process introduces personal interpretation and validation. Same information can be tranformed into grounds for both agreement and disagreement. References can serve to affirm another community‚Äôs ideas or to criticize, correct, or contrast them. This project examines how the closeness of social circles that people reference influences linguistic style, evaluative tone, and reactions to information from socially close to distant communities. Using the Reddit Hyperlink Network as a large-scale map of reference-based interaction, we analyze how communities respond to information from groups they frequently reference compared to those they rarely talk about..
