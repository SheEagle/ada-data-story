Take a Look at Reddit‚Äôs Galaxy

Welcome to the Galaxy of Reddit - a massive social networking platform, known for its vast communities (subreddits) on discussions, news, expert advice, entertainment (memes, stories), and various niche topics. In Reddit's vast digital expanse, every planet ( Subreddit) pools together likeminded thinkers from across the galaxy. These planets do not exist in isolation. They are socially connected through their inhabitants, called users constantly debating and communicating. Discussions on Reddit forums can take various forms and encompass all kinds of emotion (positive, negative, angry, appreciative, etc.). When one subreddit references another subreddit (by adding a link to a post/conversation in the target subreddit), there is information flow between communities as they interact. Some subreddits interact more frequently than others - making the planets knit together closely through social interaction. We, the geeky inhabitants of planet r/EPFL, are fascinated by these information exchange patterns and want to understand how the linguistic style of communication varies for different links within Reddit's online galaxy. To observe them, we calibrated our instruments to spy on these intra-planet links!

Our initial scan revealed two distinct styles of intra-community links. Some users embed the links to other communities' posts deep within the body of the post, while others mention it in the title itself. Stanford's Reddit Hyperlink Network record all such hyperlinks from 2014 to 2017. Using analytical tools granted by the galaxy‚Äôs data analysis overseers, ADA, we explored these hyperlinks and uncovered interesting (and sometimes unexpected) information pathways.

An Expanding the Reddit Universe

The Reddit Hyperlink Network dataset (https://snap.stanford.edu/data/soc-RedditHyperlinks.html) contains approximately 860 000 hyperlinks from one community to another, over a period of 2.5 years, with title citations forming two thirds of the total 572 000 links across 54 075 unique subreddits in the dataset. In this 2.5-year period, the weekly number of posts with mentioning other subreddits grew in a steady pace, following a strong linear trend (R2 = 0.87) (Figure 0.1), most of the posts encompassing positive or neutral sentiment. (The ratio of positive/neutral sentiment to negative was 8.35 to 1!) Furthermore, linear regression reveals a substantially steeper growth rate for positive and neutral mentions (slope = 2.39) than for negative mentions (slope = 0.24) (Figure 0.2).

That‚Äôs a nice universe expansion metric! Neutral or positive inter-community exchange is growing faster than its negativity.

We model the Reddit Hyperlink Network as a vast graph structure spanning all the subreddits and hyperlinks between them over 2.5 years. Each subreddit is a node, and each hyperlink is a directed edge from one node to another. The first step in our analysis is clustering the nodes, or subreddits, using the Leiden Algorithm which ensures a high modularity of approximately 0.42 (meaning nodes inside the same cluster have more links with each other than with nodes in other clusters) and low conductance (meaning there are fewer links going out of each cluster compared to the number of links inside it). The clustering results suggest that connectivity across Reddit is highly uneven and does not reach all nodes. Most subreddits belong to one large weakly connected cluster of 65 648 nodes, while 711 smaller clusters remain isolated from the main graph. However, strong connectivity is much more limited. Only 21 432 nodes form a single strongly connected cluster, leaving 45 563 nodes weakly attached through mostly one-way connections.

Links within this large central cluster of communities must be representative of the primary linguistic style used in inter-community links.

A Two-Stage Classification Framework for Community Reference Links

How can we define four specific groups of reference links based on community status and connection strength?

To analyze communication behavior, we use a two-stage classification framework. This system categorizes all links into four distinct groups‚ÄîIntra-Core, Intra-Periphery, Cross-Strong, and Cross-Weak‚Äîbased on the sender‚Äôs social role and the social distance between the communities.

  • Stage 1: Within-cluster Link Classification
    Within each community cluster, communities are ranked by weighted degree (total connection strength) to identify their influence levels:
    • Core Communities: The top 30% most active hubs, serving as discussion centers within the cluster.
    • Peripheral Communities: The remaining 70%, positioned at the social periphery.
    Based on this, we define the first two groups:
    ‚Ä¢ Intra-Core: Links initiated by core communities targeting other members within the same cluster.
    ‚Ä¢ Intra-Periphery: Links initiated by peripheral communities targeting other members within the same cluster.
  • Stage 2: Cross-Cluster Link Classification
    For references spanning different clusters, tie strength is measured via interaction frequency (edge weight). Using the median as a threshold, we define the remaining two groups:
    • Cross-Strong: Cross-cluster links with interaction frequency above the median, indicating close connections between the groups.
    • Cross-Weak: Cross-cluster links with interaction frequency below the median, representing occasional ‚Äúbridge‚Äù interactions between otherwise separate social domains.

Research Question 02

How does social roles and social distance within the Reddit 'ecosystem' influence the linguistic patterns and emotional tone of interactions in a peaceful context?

In the previous stage, we defined four types of links: Intra-Core, Intra-Periphery, Cross-Strong, and Cross-Weak. But before we move further with our analysis, we need to take into account sentiment type and cross-cluster differences.

Controlling conflict rate

Reddit hyperlinks can be of positive/neutral sentiment or negative sentiment. Negative references are not evenly distributed across subreddits. For example, Cross-strong links contain a disproportionately high number of negative references, while intra-periphery links contain the fewest; cross-weak and intra-core links show similar levels of positive/neutral and negative links. Pooling all references would therefore let negative links disproportionately drive the aggregate results. To control for this bias, we analyze negative and positive/neutral references separately by creating separate graph structures with links of a certain sentiment only, and repeating the process of clustering and link-type classification for each graph.

FIG 2.1

Controlling cluster specific differences

Each cluster in the Reddit galaxy consists of communities with similar topics, norms, and stylistic habits. These inherent preferences influence language. Posts from some clusters might favor long sentences, abstract concepts, and extended reasoning; others might prefer short, direct, and pragmatic communication. If a specific interaction type is dominated by certain clusters, cluster-specific style could be misattributed to social distance. To limit this bias, we use a Linear Mixed-Effects Model (LMM). Interaction type is modeled as a fixed effect, using Cross-Strong links as the reference category, or baseline with which we will compare the values for all other link types. This reduces the influence of cluster-specific language patterns and allows the fixed effects to capture relative differences associated with link type.

Research Question 04

Macro Perspective: Steady-State Structures and Conflict-Driven Reorganization in the Reddit Galaxy

So far, we have examined Reddit interactions at the micro level, focusing on signal complexity, logical style, identity markers, and emotional tone. However, a galaxy is not defined by individual signals alone. Its structure emerges from the collective motion of stars and clusters. At the macro level, we therefore ask a structural question: What defines the social architecture of the Reddit galaxy, and what happens to this architecture when conflict emerges?

All previous analyses were conducted on the global network, where positive and negative interactions were jointly aggregated. This perspective implicitly assumes that conflict behaves as a local disturbance within an otherwise stable system. To test this assumption, we deliberately reconstruct the galaxy under two extreme observational lenses: a peace network containing only positive interactions, and a conflict network containing only negative interactions.

Our analysis follows a clear, step-by-step pipeline:

  1. Extract interaction texts and sentiment labels from the raw Reddit dataset.
  2. Split the global interaction graph into a peace network (positive links only) and a conflict network (negative links only).
  3. Compute 27 linguistic features for each interaction, including readability metrics, sentence structure, pronoun usage, emotional tone, and LIWC categories.
  4. Fit linear mixed-effects models (LMMs) to estimate fixed-effect coefficients, producing high-dimensional behavioral vectors for each interaction type.
  5. Compare structures across contexts using Pearson correlation, Jaccard similarity, and feature drift analysis.

This pipeline allows us to observe the Reddit galaxy at scale: identifying its stable gravitational skeleton, and tracking how conflict perturbs or reorganizes existing orbits.

1. Initial Hypothesis: ‚ÄúMirror-Constant‚Äù Structures

In the exploratory Phase 0, we observed that positive interactions dominate: roughly 8.35 positive links per negative one. This led to the following hypothesis:

We assume that positive interactions define the Reddit galaxy‚Äôs ‚Äúgravitational skeleton,‚Äù determining which communities occupy the core, which serve as cross-cluster bridges, and which orbit at the periphery. Conflicts may induce local turbulence, but the overall skeletal structure should remain stable.

2. Technical Verification: Peace as the Galaxy‚Äôs Anchor

To test this, we compared the global network with the peace-only network:

  • Computed LMM fixed-effect coefficients for each link, resulting in 27-dimensional high-dimensional behavior vectors.
  • Calculated Pearson correlations between corresponding links in the peace and global networks.
  • Observed near-perfect alignment: peripheral links (Intra-Periphery) reached correlations up to 0.99, with core links similarly aligned.

Examining the conflict-only network reveals that the mirror-constant assumption breaks down. Conflict does not just amplify existing patterns‚Äîit generates a new social gravity, reshaping the galaxy‚Äôs landscape.

To analyze structural reorganization under conflict, we employed a combination of set theory and statistical comparison:

5. Conflict vs. Peace: Macro Patterns

Peace Defines the Rules

Positive interactions form the framework for clusters, cores, and bridges, which remains robust under normal conditions.

Conflict Drives Drift

Negative interactions trigger structural drift; peripheral clusters destabilize first, and cross-cluster links may lose authority.

Dynamic Role Shifts

High-traffic core links are strong under peace but vulnerable in conflict due to their reliance on positive interactions.

Reshuffling

Conflict elevates peripheral and opportunistic clusters, challenging the established hierarchy.

Conclusion: The Reddit galaxy is a dynamic equilibrium system. Peace defines the steady-state social order, while conflict drives localized reorganization. Influence under calm conditions does not guarantee influence under pure antagonism‚Äîsocial gravity is context-dependent, and roles can invert quickly.
